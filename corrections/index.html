Corrections for Games from Irving Chernev’s “Practical Chess Endings”

For All publications 1961 to the Present

Simon & Schuster 1961, (still printed on-demand as of 2019) New York; Faber & Faber Limited 1962, Reprinted 1973, London & Dover Publications, Inc., 1961, New York.

NOTE: All three publications had the identical format, font style and size - even chess figurines alongside the page numbers--all had used the original - believe Simon & Schuster.  

NOTE All moves shown here with Bold for AN notations are only shown for emphasis. Moves  WILL NOT BE SHOWN in bold in the final re-publication text of “Practical Chess Endings” Only Here.

Game No. 16  (page 27) NOTE: Line five: original move 3 PxP . should have been  2 PxP.  AN becomes 2 bxa3, etc.

Game No. 23: (page 34) NOTE: Line five Black’s original move  6 . . . K-R1 should have been      6 . . . K-R8. AN becomes 6 . . . Ka1, etc.

Game No. 26: (page 37) NOTE: Original Black move in 2nd column-Line 4  13.  . . K-R1!  Should have been 13.  . . K-R8!; AN becomes 13 . . .         Ka1!

Game No. 37: (page 48)  NOTE:  In 2nd paragraph Black’s move 3 KxP, K-Kt2 should have been 3. . . K-Q2. (White’s King is on  b6!!) AN becomes  3 Kxb6, Kd7

Game No. 51: (page 62) NOTE: Line 12, White’s move 6  K-B1 was an error in the original publications.  Should have been 6  K-B8.   AN becomes 6 Kc8

Game No. 59: (page 70)   NOTE: Line 10 Black’s move 2 . . . K-R4 was an error.  Should have been 2 . . . K-R5.  (Black’s own Pawn is resting on R4.).  AN becomes  2 . . .  Kh4.

Game No. 74 (page 85) NOTE: In third paragraph White’s move 2  P-B6  puts Black’s King in check.  Should have been:  2 P-B6ch. AN move becomes:  2 f6+ (very temporarily!)

Game No. 113 (page 125) NOTE: For other defenses C] White’s move # 2 P-B8(Q) was in error.  Should have been:  2 P-Q8(Q). AN move becomes:  2 d8+.

Game No. 122 (page 134) NOTE: In first sentence too many “the”s -- Remove one of them.  In second paragraph second sentence: If for example 3 K-Q3, should have been 3 K-Q6. AN becomes  3 Kd6.

Game No. 139 (page 152) NOTE: Although it doesn’t play a factor, in the third sentence (ninth line) move:  4 B-Q6 was in error (the Bishop is white).  It should have been 4 B-Q5. AN becomes 4 Bd5.

Game No. 149 (page 162) NOTE: First paragraph, third line; and third paragraph, third line both show QB8.  The Queening square is in the King Bishops’ file, not Queens! Both should read KB8. AN becomes f8 for both cases.

Game No. 157 (page 170) NOTE: At paragraph four - first sentence: On 5 . . . K-Kt4 5 P-R7, P-K5ch    6 KxB, and the Pawn cannot be over-taken.  Should have been: On 5 . . . K-Kt4 6 P-R7, P-K5ch    7 KxB, and the Pawn cannot be over-taken.  AN becomes: On 5 . . . Kg5 6 h7, e4+      7  Kxd4, and the Pawn cannot be over-taken.

Game No. 165 (page 178) NOTE: Fourth paragraph first line states::: There was no hope in 2 . . . RR3ch 4 B-B6ch, etc.  Should have been: There was no hope in 2 . . . RR3ch 3 B-B6ch, etc.

AN becomes: There was no hope in 2 . . . Ra6+ 3 Bc6+, etc.

Game No. 166 (page 178) NOTE:  Line three for A] 1 P-Kt7, B-K6ch, etc. is in error.  Should have been A] 1 P-Kt7, B-K3ch, etc..  AN becomes A] 1 g7, Be6+.

Game No. 174 (page 187) NOTE: There was a mistake in all original publications, especially for K] 5  . . .Q-QB6 6 B-K3ch which cannot win the  Queen at this position.  It should have been: K] 5  . . .Q-QB6 6 B-Kt4ch.  AN becomes: K] 5  . . .Qc3 6. Bb4+ winning the Queen.
